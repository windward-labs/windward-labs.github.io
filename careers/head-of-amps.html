Head of Amps

Responsibilities

  • Lead the development and growth of Amps, our flagship Farcaster miniapp
  • Talk to users, develop and ship product features, and work with our engineering team to scale the product

About the product

Amps is a two-sided marketplace to buy and sell engagement. Launched on Farcaster, we've gone from 0->1 and proved demand for the concept. Now we're looking to double down and build a best-in-class social experience to help users earn their first dollar onchain.

About you

  • You have experience shipping products that users love
  • You're deeply immersed in Farcaster and stay up-to-date on the latest community products and protocol developments
  • You're looking for a proven idea that you can help scale -- and benefit in the upside of your work

Tech Stack

  • Backend: Node.js, Postgres, Redis, Solidity
  • Infrastructure: Heroku, Vercel, Ethereum
  • Frontend: Typescript React
  • Design: Figma, v0

Culture and Compensation

  • High-integrity, low-ego team.
  • Flexible hours within a US based timezone. We work in Pacific Time most weeks.
  • Salary USD $100k – $120k plus token grant
  • Health/vision/dental.

About the role

You will be the public face of Amps and have significant autonomy over the product roadmap. You will work directly with our team to ship features and drive the direction of the product.

This is a great opportunity for someone who wants to scale an early stage product with a proven set of early users.

Remote-US preferred.

About Windward Labs

Windward Labs is a creative agency that delivers software and physical experiences to elevate the next generation of onchain creators. We are deeply optimistic and believe that a small group of people can make an outsized impact on the world through their work.

Interview Process

  1. Phone Call: 30 minute conversation with Phil to discuss the role, your background, the any questions you may have
  2. Technical Call: 30 minute conversation with Jeff to learn about your technical background and area of expertise
  3. Work Trial: 1 week paid work trial to ship real code in a production setting and learn about how you work and solve problems.
